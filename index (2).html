Learn the Science Behind

DNA and RNA carry genetic information. DNA contains the instructions for making proteins. RNA transfers these instructions to the ribosome for protein synthesis.

Nucleotide Bases: DNA bases: Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Cytosine (C), Guanine (G). RNA replaces Thymine with Uracil (U). Specific pairing rules: A with T (U in RNA), and C with G due to hydrogen bonding.

Introns are non-coding segments in mRNA, removed during processing. Exons are the coding segments translated into protein.

The 5' cap and poly-A tail protect the mRNA and help in translation initiation.

Codons and Anticodons: A codon is a sequence of three nucleotides in mRNA that corresponds to a specific amino acid. A tRNA anticodon binds to a codon to deliver the correct amino acid.

Ribosomes: Ribosomes are cellular machinery that reads mRNA codons and helps form a chain of amino acids (polypeptide) to build proteins.

Amino Acids: Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins. Each amino acid is coded by a specific codon, and the chain folds into a functional protein.
